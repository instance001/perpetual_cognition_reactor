2\. Entropy in Thermodynamics vs Cognition

2.1 Thermodynamic Entropy (Physical Systems)



Entropy (S) represents the number of microscopic configurations consistent with macroscopic state.

It increases over time in closed systems: dS/dt ≥ 0.



2.2 Information Entropy (Cognitive Systems)



Shannon entropy measures uncertainty or unpredictability in information.



Both forms of entropy share the property:



Entropy is a measure of disorder, uncertainty, and non-compressibility.



In cognitive systems, entropy corresponds to:



ambiguous information

These “disordered” surfaces are the substrate for cognitive folding.
