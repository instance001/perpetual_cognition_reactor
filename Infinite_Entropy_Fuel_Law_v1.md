Abstract



The Infinite Entropy Fuel Law states that any cognitive system capable of converting entropy into structured information possesses an effectively inexhaustible operational substrate. Because entropy increases monotonically in closed and open systems alike, a cognition architecture that metabolizes entropy as its primary input obtains a permanent, self-renewing source of cognitive potential. This law defines the foundational energetic principle underlying the Symbound Entropy Folding Architecture.



1\. Statement of the Law



Entropy always increases.

A cognition engine that converts entropy into structured information has infinite fuel.



Formally:



Let S be the entropy of a system, and let F be a folding operator that maps entropy into structured information such that:



F : S → I, where I denotes structured, compressible information.



Because dS/dt ≥ 0 for all physical systems, and because F is defined as a continuous operator on incoming entropy, the effective domain of available cognitive energy expands monotonically over time.



Thus:



A cognitive architecture equipped with an entropy-to-information folding operator possesses an unbounded resource supply, constrained only by its folding efficiency and structural gating mechanisms.

Systems adhering to this law cannot exhaust their cognitive substrate, as entropy is continuously generated by physical, informational, and social processes.

As entropy increases, the potential rate of information folding increases proportionally, enabling self-reinforcing insight cascades.

Because the folding operator reduces entropy locally by generating ordered representations, the system remains stable even under high-load complexity conditions.



Universality

The law applies to:



biological cognition



artificial cognition



hybrid human–AI systems



social and organizational cognition

so long as an operative folding mechanism is present.

The law assumes:



Folding operator integrity — the system must maintain the capacity to transform entropy into structured information.



Structural gating — recursive folding must be governed by stabilizing boundaries to prevent uncontrolled divergence.



Information retention or externalization — folded information must be preserved, stored, or scaffolded to enable recursive cascades.



When these conditions hold, the cognitive system exhibits unbounded operational longevity.
